# A Day in the Life of a Contractor

## 6:00 AM
- Check weather risk by site and decide start delays or sequencing changes.
- Confirm which sites can open safely and which crews are on standby.

## 6:30 AM
- Open site gates and verify safety setup (power, signage, PPE readiness).
- Confirm supervisor coverage and backup leaders for each active zone.

## 7:00 AM
- Workers arrive; verify attendance flow and first-wave check-ins.
- Detect no-shows and decide whether to reassign or call reserve labor.

## 7:30 AM
- Balance labor across projects based on urgent work packages.
- Approve first worker transfers between sites.

## 8:00 AM
- Review material delivery schedule; prioritize unload slots and cranes.
- Decide if morning tasks need resequencing due to supply timing.

## 9:00 AM
- Validate subcontractor readiness and permit dependencies.
- Resolve trade conflicts (electrical vs concrete vs framing access).

## 10:00 AM
- Review productivity vs plan and decide acceleration actions.
- Approve overtime risk only for critical path activities.

## 11:00 AM
- Inspect incident/near-miss alerts and trigger corrective actions.
- Decide if any zone requires temporary stop-work.

## 12:00 PM
- Lunch window: maintain critical operations while rotating crews.
- Confirm afternoon manpower availability.

## 1:00 PM
- Check concrete pour or major install readiness.
- Decide go/no-go based on weather, crew count, and equipment status.

## 2:00 PM
- Handle equipment breakdowns and route replacements.
- Decide whether to rent emergency equipment or resequence tasks.

## 3:00 PM
- Reconcile attendance anomalies (duplicate punches, missing check-outs).
- Approve late worker transfers to rescue blocked activities.

## 4:00 PM
- Process rain delay or heat safety response.
- Decide partial shutdown vs protected continuation by zone.

## 5:00 PM
- End-of-day check-out wave begins; ensure complete attendance capture.
- Confirm labor coding to the correct site/project/cost bucket.

## 6:00 PM
- Review daily production vs target and exception reasons.
- Decide recovery plan for tomorrow’s shortfalls.

## 7:00 PM
- Finalize supervisor reports and unresolved issues handoff.
- Approve urgent procurement requests for next day starts.

## 8:00 PM
- Review payroll-impacting attendance events and overtime approvals.
- Lock daily operations summary and tomorrow’s first-shift priorities.
